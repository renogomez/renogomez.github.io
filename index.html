cvTraffic

Traffic sensor to count and classify vehicles using computer vision.

This was a project developed with the Colombian Administrative Department of Science, Technology and Innovation; COLCIENCIAS. We developed a traffic sensor to count and classify vehicles. The main challenge was to avoid errors caused by the people who work selling products at road intersections. You can see them right there on the street, even during green lights.

This is how our sensor was integrated with other projects to anilise vehicle tranffic. We integrated the sensor with traffic controllers and geographic information systems to visualize traffic state in control centers.

Colombian Armed Conflict

Interact with Colombian Armed Conflict data from Centro Memoria.

This is a sideproject about warlike actions data visualization in the context of Colombian armed conflict. In 2016 I sent this example to Centro Nacional de Memoria Histórica but they told me a new website will be released. I don't like the result they have finally published at 'Rutas del Conflicto' so I will continue this project with the data published in 2017. I agree with Lisa Charlotte Rost when she says we need to create visualizations to make people feel empathy about this topics, because it becomes harder no to act, harder no to help, in what we both (me and Centro Memoria) still fail.

  • Date: Nov. 2016
  • Client: Sideproject
  • Category: information visualization

Geo Tweets

Tweets visualization according to topic and geolocation.

This is a project for tweets visualization, developed at Bios in order to practice some technologies. An algorithm qualifies tweets witten in spanish as positive (green), negative (red) o neutral (gray). You can also search up to four (4) different topics in order to see places where people is tweeting them the most. You can filter topics and feelings.

I designed the interface and lead the team for project implementation. I developed d3.js and leaflet related components.

  • Date: June. 2017
  • Client: Internal project at BIOS
  • Category: information visualization
